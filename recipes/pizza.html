Pizza

Pizza is a popular dish of Italian origin, consisting of a round, flattened base of leavened wheat-based dough topped with tomatoes, cheese, and often various other ingredients (such as anchovies, mushrooms, onions, olives, pineapple, meat, etc.) baked at a high temperature, traditionally in a wood-fired oven. The dough is typically flavored with olive oil, salt, and sometimes sugar. Pizza is enjoyed worldwide and comes in numerous styles and variations, with the Margherita pizza, topped with tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella, fresh basil, and olive oil, being one of the most classic and well-loved varieties.

Ingredients

  • Flour - 2 cups
  • Water - 1 cup
  • Yeast - 1 teaspoon
  • Salt - 1/2 teaspoon
  • Tomato Sauce - 1/2 cup
  • Mozzarella Cheese - 1 to 1.5 cups (shredded)
  • Olive Oil - 2 tablespoons
  • Oregano - 1/2 teaspoon

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, mix 2 cups of flour, 1 teaspoon of yeast, and 1/2 teaspoon of salt.
  2. Add 1 cup of warm water and mix well until a dough forms. If necessary, add more flour until the dough is not sticky.
  3. Place the dough on a floured surface and knead for about 10 minutes.
  4. Place the dough in a bowl covered with a damp cloth and let it rest in a warm place for 1 to 2 hours, or until it doubles in size.
  5. Preheat the oven to 475°F (245°C).
  6. Roll out the dough on a pizza tray or flat surface.
  7. Spread 1/2 cup of tomato sauce evenly over the dough.
  8. Sprinkle 1 to 1.5 cups of shredded mozzarella cheese over the sauce.
  9. Drizzle 2 tablespoons of olive oil over the cheese.
  10. Sprinkle 1/2 teaspoon of oregano on top.
  11. Bake in the preheated oven for about 12-15 minutes or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.
  12. Remove from oven, let it cool for a few minutes, slice, and enjoy!
